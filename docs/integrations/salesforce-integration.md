# Salesforce Integration

Connect Document360 with Salesforce to surface knowledge base content directly within your CRM workflows.

---

## Overview

The Salesforce integration lets support and sales teams access relevant documentation articles without leaving Salesforce, reducing context-switching and improving customer interactions.

---

## Prerequisites

- A Salesforce account with admin permissions
- Document360 project admin access
- Salesforce API credentials (Connected App client ID and secret)

---

## Setup Steps

1. Navigate to **Settings → Integrations → Salesforce** in Document360.
2. Click **Connect with Salesforce**.
3. Enter your Salesforce **instance URL**, **client ID**, and **client secret**.
4. Authorize the connection.
5. Map Document360 categories to Salesforce objects (Cases, Contacts, Leads).
6. Click **Save**.

---

## Features

- **In-App Article Suggestions** – Surface relevant articles inside Salesforce case views.
- **Case Deflection** – Automatically suggest knowledge base articles when a case is created.
- **Sync Contacts** – Associate documentation feedback with Salesforce contacts.
- **Activity Logging** – Log article views as Salesforce activities for reporting.

---

## Use Cases

- Help support agents find answers quickly from within a case.
- Reduce ticket volume by suggesting self-service articles to customers.
- Track which knowledge base articles are most referenced during sales cycles.

---

## Best Practices

- Keep article titles concise and descriptive for better suggestion accuracy.
- Regularly review case-deflection metrics to refine article coverage.
- Use Salesforce profiles to control which teams can access integrated articles.

---

## Summary

The Salesforce integration brings your knowledge base into the heart of your CRM, empowering teams to resolve issues faster and deliver better customer experiences.
